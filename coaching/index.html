How I Hit 1500+ Subscribers

Started dreaming of YouTube at 13, but didn't take action. I thought that my Samsung Galaxy J5 Prime wasn't good enough. Got obsessed with the idea that an iPhone 7 was the key to everything (It wasn't).

Everything changed when I found self-improvement content (Hormozi, Hamza, Iman Gadzhi). Once, while listening to a podcast by Alex Hormozi about Atomic Habits, something clicked. I stopped overthinking and just hit record.

It was rough at first. My English wasn't perfect, and my editing was super basic. I uploaded 100 videos, and only hit 150 subs. That's when I knew I had to change something.

Thanks to Hamza, I discovered Skool, joined communities, got a couple of mentors (first Jan, then Hayden), and kept grinding. I started paying for mentorship, investing in my learning, and just following their advice. But the results weren't as fast as I thought they'd be.

Then I learned something: if your mentor is someone you don't actually want to be like, maybe they're not the right fit for you. I found my style eventually and just kept grinding.

Now I have over 1,500 subs, but it's just the beginning. This journey wasn't about luck or some magic formula. It was about sticking with it, learning from mistakes, and making changes along the way.
